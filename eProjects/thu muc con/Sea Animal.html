Sea Animal

Electric EEL

  

Size: More than 8 feet (2,4m).

Diet: crabs ,freshwater shrimp ,fishes and crustaceans.

Range: murky pool ,lower Amazon and Orinoco River basins in South America.

Did You Know? "Electric eels can produce up to 600 volts of electricity".

 
     

GREEN MORAY EEL

  

Size: 8 feet (2.4m) and 65 pound (30kg).

Diet: fishes, crabs, shrimp, octopuses, and squid.

Range: found in western Atlantic , cracks and crevices, along rocky shorelines, and in coral reefs.

Did You Know? "Green moray eels are often feared and mistaken for sea serpents."

 
     

LONGSNOUT SEAHORSE

  

Size: typically range from 2 to 4 inches, with a maximum size of 6 inches.

Diet: Seahorses primarily eat larval shrimp and amphipods.

Range: Western Atlantic Ocean.

Did You Know? "Seahorses give birth to anywhere from five to 2,500 young at a time."

 

 
     

SPOTFIN PORCUPINEFISH

  

Size: A spotfin porcupinefish can grow to a maximum length of 3 feet.

Diet: Sea urchins, gastropods, crustaceans.

Range: Circumtropical.

Did You Know? "The porcupinefish gets its name from the sharp spines that stick out when it inflates with water."

 

 
     

WOLF EEL

  

Size: The wolf eel can reach nearly 8 feet long.

Diet: The wolf eel eats hard-shelled animals such as snails, clams, crabs, and sea urchins.

Range: This fish lives in the North Pacific from the Sea of Japan, to islands off the coast of Alaska, to the coast of southern California.

Did You Know? "The wolf eel has been known to be friendly to divers, even gently eating out of their hands!"

 
     

BLACKTIP REEF SHARK

  

Size: These sharks can grow up to 6 feet long.

Diet: Blacktip Reef sharks primarily feed on reef fish but sometimes eat crustaceans, cephalopods and mollusks.

Range: Blacktip Reef sharks are commonly found along the coastlines of Pacific regions, such as Thailand, Japan ,...

Did You Know? "These sharks sometimes jump fully out of the water during feeding frenzies."

 

 
     

BONNETHEAD SHARK

  

Size: averaging 30 to 48 inches ,with a maximum reported total length of 59 inches.,weigh up to 24 pounds.

Diet: Bonnethead Sharks feed primarily on crustaceans, but they also eat clams, octopuses and small fish.

Range: found in tropical and subtropical waters on both coasts of North America, it lives in estuaries and shallow bays with mud or sand bottoms and in coral reefs.

Did You Know? "The bonnethead is one of the smallest members of the hammerhead genus."
     

HORN SHARK

 

Size: 38–47 inches long.

Diet: The horn shark eats small fish and invertebrates.

Range: found inhabiting the coastal areas from Southern California to the Gulf of California and areas around Ecuador and Peru.

Did You Know? "This shark is named for the venomous horns on its dorsal fins."

 
     

LARGETOOTH SAWFISH

  

Size: They can reach a maximum length of 19.7 ft, and have been reported to attain 23 feet.

Diet: Largetooth sawfish prey upon small fishes, prawns, and other crustaceans. Fishes dominate the diet as they grow.

Range: Circumtropical in coastal waters, and in some river systems, including the famed Lake Nicaragua population.

Did You Know? "This species is tolerant of a wide-range of salinities, occurring from freshwater to seawater (euryhaline)."
